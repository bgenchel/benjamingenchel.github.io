Production/Composition

Pre-emptive title: Botty

A work in progress piece written and produced by me with help and guidance from my teacher, Cody Farwell.

Pre-emptively called 'First', so named because it was/is the first song I worked on with my teacher, Cody Farwell (Goodnight Cody). To me, the composition inconsistent; though it keeps the same key throughout, the transitions and sections reflect that this track was really more of a playground for attempting and demonstrating production techniques with composition applied as an afterthought.

A short piece I wrote and produced as part of a beat Cypher for UC San Diego's Producers and Remixers Organization.

A sound collage I made as a final project for a class in the history of electronic music. Each section represents a different era in electronic music history, concluding with a reproduction of 'Bicycle Built for Two,', an homage to Max Matthew's work at Bell Labs in 1950.

This is the first piece I ever produced using Ableton Live, and really what I would consider my first production, since my prior attempts were haphazardly thrown together using demo software. As a result, this is the first piece where I didn't just write notes down to be played in a sequencer, but designed and controlled sounds and effects at a somewhat fine grain.

This piece was written/produced for an assignment given in my electronic music history class. The assignment was very simple, just record sounds and pitch them up and down. I however, decided to showboat and so made a passion project out of it.

In line with the assignment, all the sounds sans voice samples (taken from the audio for Goku's super saiyan 3 transformation from Akira Torriyama's DragonBall Z) were sounds recorded from around my dorm using the microphone on my years old hand-me-down HP laptop.

The melody and key for the piece were inspired and provided respectively by the fundemental frequencies and accompanying harmonics from a recording of a giant fan being used to mitigate the unpleasant scents coming from UCSD's sewers; a true 'look on the bright side' situation. Other samples used include me cracking my knuckles, slamming a desk drawer and, hitting a pot with a wooden spoon.
